Cyber security

Personal cybersecurity is crucial in today's digital landscape, where our lives are increasingly dependant on the technology we use. With sensitive information—such as bank details, personal messages, and health records—stored online, the risk of identity theft, financial loss, and privacy breaches is ever-present. Maintaining good cyber security practices will protect personal data and keep you safer online. By prioritizing cybersecurity, we can safeguard our digital identities and navigate the online world with greater confidence and peace of mind.

Learn

Passwords

For stronger and more secure passwords :

  • Use passwords that are at least 16 characters or longer. The longer the password the longer it takes to crack by brute force.
  • Use a random string of mixed-case letters, numbers and symbols and make them unique. A password manager can help create and store these so you dont't forget.
  • Only share passwords with people you can trust and never leave passwords written down for others to see.

2FA

Two-factor authentication means that whatever application or service you're logging in to is double-checking that the request is really coming from you by confirming the login with you through a separate venue.

2FA is essential to web security because it immediately neutralizes the risks associated with compromised passwords. If a password is hacked, guessed, or even phished, that's no longer enough to give an intruder access: without approval at the second factor, a password alone is useless. If a website or application has the ability to enable 2fa it is always good practice to do so.

Software

Tips for downaloading software safely and securely :

  • Only download from official websites, app stores, or trusted developers.
  • Check for authenticity and look for digital signatures, official logos, and contact information on the website.
  • Install and use anti-malware software to scan files for malware as they are downloaded.
  • Avoid suspicious websites that claim to offer free versions of trusted software may include malicious code.
  • Use a virus scanner to detect and remove viruses and other types of malicious software.

Phishing

Phishing is when attackers send scam emails (or text messages) that contain links to malicious websites. The websites may contain malware (such as ransomware) which can sabotage systems and organisations. Or they might be designed to trick users into revealing sensitive information (such as passwords), or transferring money. Ways to prevent this are :

  • Familiarise yourself for what a phishing scams looks like.
  • Use strong passwords & enable two-factor authentication.
  • Understand that companies will never ask for details directly over email, text or calls.
  • Get free anti-phishing add-ons.

Network

Ways to help protect your network include :

  • Change the default password on your router and use a strong password instead
  • Check if your router has the option to turn encryption.Turning this setting on can help provide extra security to your network.
  • Use a VPN for additional network security. VPNs encrypt your data so that a hacker can't tell what you are doing online or where you are located.
  • Keep your router firmware up to date. Older firmware will have vulnerabilities that hackers can exploit so keeping up to date can prevent this

Accounts

  • Limit Personal Information: Be mindful of the information you share on social media and public forums. The less personal information you share, the harder it is for someone to guess your passwords or security questions.
  • Secure Your Devices: Keep your computer and mobile devices secure with antivirus software and regular updates. Also, use device encryption if available.
  • Log Out of Accounts: Especially on shared or public devices, make sure to log out of accounts when you're done.
  • Monitor Account Activity: Regularly check your accounts for unauthorized transactions or changes. Many services offer alerts for unusual activity.

Settings

Some settings that are useful include :

  • Set your accounts to private and review the default privacy settings so you can control who sees what on your accounts.
  • Use the block function to protect yourself from spam accounts and unwanted followers/interactions.
  • Turn off auto login to prevent people from instantly accessing your accounts

Data

  • Check if the site is secure. Before entering personal information into a website, take a look at the top of your browser. If there is a lock symbol and the URL begins with “https,” that means the site is secure.
  • Be careful not to share personal details such as your date of birth, mother's maiden name, home address, previous address, employer, birthplace or school details. Banks often use these pieces of personal information to verify your identity and all of them are potentially valuable to criminals
  • Disable cookies. Cookies store the information when you visit a site so they can be retrieved and used to, for example, to recall your preferences or remember actions you have taken (like adding items to a shopping basket).
